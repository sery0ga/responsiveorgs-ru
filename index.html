This project is my attempt to show the beauty of organisations where people are trusted to use their talents to have their job done.

There is no invention on my side. All I did is I put together many scattered pieces of information from articles, books and talks. Sometimes it was easy (like FAVI), sometimes - not (like Github).

The website consists of three sections:

  • Case Studies contains examples of responsive organisations
  • Values contains a set of basic values which in my opinion are necessary conditions for any responsive organisation
  • Approaches contains a collection of methods, approaches and tools used by at least 2-3 organisations

Why responsive?

There are many names for organisations of the future. Rob Collins calls them Wiki. Dave Gray - Connected. Niels Pflaeging - Beta. Frederic Laloux - Teal. Stelio Verzera - Liquid. Probably the most well-known name is Networked.

Some names I like more, some - less. For example, Connected and Networked put too much focus on structure and less - on humans. Beta and Teal require to know the supportive models to understand these definitions. I prefer the name Responsive because it's more descriptive:

  • Organisational structure responds to outside signals and transforms to attend market needs
  • Organisations are responsive and attentive to folks' need (thanks to Bob Marshall). All stakeholders are included: employees, customers, shareholders, community, environment.
